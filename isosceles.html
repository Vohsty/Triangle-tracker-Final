THIS IS AN ISOSCELES TRIANGLE

In geometry, an isosceles triangle is a triangle that has two sides of equal length.
Sometimes it is specified as having exactly two sides of equal length, and sometimes
as having at least two sides of equal length, the latter version thus including the
equilateral triangle as a special case. Examples of isosceles triangles include the
isosceles right triangle, the golden triangle, and the faces of bipyramids and certain
Catalan solids.
